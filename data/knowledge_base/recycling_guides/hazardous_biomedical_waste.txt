HAZARDOUS AND BIOMEDICAL WASTE DISPOSAL GUIDE FOR INDIA
Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules 2016 + SWM Rules 2016

=== DOMESTIC HAZARDOUS WASTE — RED BIN ===
Waste that is toxic, flammable, corrosive, or reactive — never mix with regular waste.

MEDICINES AND PHARMACEUTICAL WASTE:
What: Expired medicines, unused prescription drugs, antibiotics, syrups, ointments, injections
Why hazardous: Pharmaceutical contamination in groundwater; antibiotic resistance from improper disposal
NEVER: Flush down toilet (contaminates water supply), throw in green/blue bin, burn
Correct disposal:
1. Keep in original container if possible
2. Place in RED bin for collection OR
3. Return to nearest pharmacy — many pharmacies in India accept expired medicines
4. CPCB "Safe Disposal of Unwanted Medicines" programme: registered collection centres
5. In Bangalore: Apollo, Manipal, Fortis hospitals have medicine take-back points

SYRINGES AND SHARPS:
Used by diabetics, patients requiring injections at home
NEVER put loose needles in any bin — injury to Pourakarmikas, waste handlers
Correct disposal:
1. "Clip and cap" method: Use a syringe clipper (Rs. 80–150, available at medical stores) — clips the needle safely
2. Or: place used syringe (capped) in a HARD plastic bottle (500 ml cold drink bottle works) — fill and tape shut
3. Label bottle "SHARPS — HANDLE WITH CARE"
4. Deposit at nearest hospital waste management station or pharmacy

BATTERIES — ALL TYPES:
Why hazardous: Lead, cadmium, mercury, lithium — all leach into soil and groundwater
AA/AAA/C/D (Alkaline): RED bin or DWCC
9V batteries: RED bin
Button cells (watch batteries): Jeweller shops, DWCC, e-waste centres
Lithium-ion (phone, laptop): MANUFACTURER take-back only — fire/explosion risk if punctured; NEVER crush or puncture
Lead-acid car/inverter battery: ALWAYS return to battery dealer/mechanic — they pay Rs. 200–500 back; mandatory take-back programme in India
Rechargeable NiMH/NiCd (older phones, toys): DWCC or e-waste recycler

CFL BULBS AND FLUORESCENT TUBES:
Contain mercury (3–5 mg per CFL) — extremely toxic if released
If CFL breaks at home:
1. Open windows, leave room for 15 minutes (ventilation)
2. Do NOT vacuum — spreads mercury dust
3. Pick up large pieces with gloves or stiff paper
4. Use damp paper towel for small fragments
5. Place in zip-lock bag or sealed container
6. Label "BROKEN CFL — CONTAINS MERCURY"
7. Take to DWCC or call BBMP 1533
Correct disposal of intact CFLs:
- BBMP DWCC — accepts CFLs and fluorescent tubes
- Electronics shops: Philips, Osram, Syska dealers take back old bulbs
- First Saturday e-waste drive at BBMP ward offices
- NEVER in regular bin, NEVER break intentionally

PAINT AND CHEMICAL CONTAINERS:
Paints (oil-based), varnish, thinner, turpentine, wood preservatives, adhesives:
- Let container dry completely — expose to air until contents solidify
- Once solidified: place in RED bin
- Liquid contents: Use completely, do not dump in drain (blocks and pollutes)
- Empty containers with dried paint: RED bin (some DWCCs accept)
Water-based paints: Less hazardous; let dry completely, then RED/blue bin

PESTICIDE AND INSECTICIDE CONTAINERS:
Highly toxic — do NOT reuse containers for any purpose
- Triple rinse empty containers with water (rinse water can be used as diluted pesticide on plants)
- Puncture or crush the container so it cannot be reused
- Place in RED bin
- Never burn pesticide containers — toxic fumes
- BBMP and Agriculture Department organise periodic collection drives for farm pesticide containers

MOTOR OIL AND LUBRICANTS:
Never pour down drain or on ground
Used motor oil: Take to mechanic/petrol station — they sell it to oil recyclers
Oil filters: Metal + oil — take to mechanic for proper disposal
Brake fluid, coolant: Mechanic for disposal; do not mix with other waste

=== BIOMEDICAL WASTE (HEALTHCARE) ===
Bio-Medical Waste Management Rules 2016 govern disposal from hospitals, clinics, laboratories.

COLOUR CODING FOR BIOMEDICAL WASTE:
YELLOW bag: Anatomical waste, human tissues, placenta, organs, contaminated waste (autoclave then landfill)
RED bag: Recyclable contaminated waste — IV bottles, tubing, catheters (after disinfection → recycler)
WHITE/TRANSLUCENT: Sharps — needles, blades, broken glass (puncture-proof container → autoclave → shredder)
BLUE/TRANSPARENT: Glassware — glass slides, broken glass from lab (puncture-proof box)

FOR HOME HEALTHCARE PATIENTS:
If someone at home generates healthcare waste (wound dressings, bandages, IV equipment):
1. Collect in a separate RED bin
2. Tie securely in RED bag or heavy-duty garbage bag
3. Hand to nearest private clinic/hospital — they must accept under Bio-Medical Waste Rules
4. Do NOT mix with household waste
5. Soiled bandages and cotton: wrap in newspaper, seal, RED bin — okay for minimal quantities

=== SANITARY WASTE ===
Under SWM Rules 2016:
Sanitary napkins, tampons, diapers (adult and baby):

Correct disposal:
1. Wrap used sanitary napkin/diaper in the wrapper of the new one, or in newspaper
2. Seal with tape if possible
3. Place in RED bin (domestic hazardous/reject bin)
4. Do NOT flush (causes sewer blockages — costs BWSSB crores in clearance)
5. Do NOT throw in green or blue bin (contaminates recyclables, spreads pathogens)

Why RED bin? Sanitary waste contains body fluids (biohazardous) and plastic/superabsorbent polymer (non-recyclable). It goes to specially designed landfill cells or incineration.

Current problem in India: Most citizens don't wrap sanitary waste, creating severe hazard for Pourakarmikas who handle waste by hand. Please ALWAYS wrap before disposal.

=== CONSTRUCTION WASTE IN HOUSEHOLDS ===
Renovation debris — treated separately from household waste:
What: Broken tiles, rubble, cement, bricks, plaster, wood (painted), steel reinforcement
NEVER mix with household MSW (clogs collection vehicles, harms Pourakarmikas)
Disposal:
1. Hire a builder to collect and transport to BBMP-designated C&D waste site
2. BBMP has C&D waste plants at Kannahalli, Yelahanka, Bommasandra
3. For small quantities (minor repairs): Contact BBMP ward office for guidance
4. Metal reinforcement (steel rods): Sell to scrap dealer — high value
5. Broken tiles and bricks: Can be reused as garden pathway, pot drainage, filling

=== SUMMARY — RED BIN ITEMS (QUICK REFERENCE) ===
✓ Expired/unused medicines
✓ Syringes and sharps (in hard container)
✓ All batteries (AA, lithium-ion, lead-acid, button cells)
✓ CFL and fluorescent bulbs (intact — handle gently)
✓ Paint tins (empty/dried)
✓ Pesticide containers (triple rinsed)
✓ Sanitary napkins, diapers, tampons (wrapped in newspaper)
✓ Soiled bandages and wound dressings (wrapped)
✓ Cosmetics past expiry date
✓ Thermocol (expanded polystyrene)
✓ Laminated packaging, metallic pouches, blister packs
✓ Carbon paper, thermal paper (ATM receipts)
✓ Broken crockery, ceramics (wrap in newspaper — SHARP)
✓ Hair (large quantities — such as from salon)
✓ Cigarette butts (nicotine is toxic to soil microbes)
