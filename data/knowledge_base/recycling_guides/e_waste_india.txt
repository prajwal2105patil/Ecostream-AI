E-WASTE (ELECTRONIC WASTE) DISPOSAL GUIDE FOR INDIA
Under E-Waste (Management) Rules 2022

WHAT IS E-WASTE?
Electronic waste includes: Mobile phones, laptops, computers, monitors, keyboards, printers, tablets, cameras, chargers, batteries, TVs, refrigerators, washing machines, ACs, LED bulbs, fluorescent lights, microwave ovens

WHY E-WASTE IS DANGEROUS:
E-waste contains hazardous materials:
- Lead (in CRT screens): Damages nervous system
- Mercury (in LCD screens, fluorescent bulbs): Toxic to brain and kidneys
- Cadmium (in batteries): Carcinogenic
- Chromium: Causes DNA damage
- Brominated flame retardants: Disrupt hormones
NEVER burn or break e-waste - toxic fumes and leaching

LEGAL REQUIREMENTS IN INDIA (E-Waste Rules 2022):
- Consumers MUST deposit e-waste at authorized collection points
- Manufacturers MUST provide free take-back programs
- Improper disposal: Fine up to Rs. 1 lakh for bulk generators

HOW TO DISPOSE E-WASTE IN INDIA:

OPTION 1: MANUFACTURER TAKE-BACK (Free)
- Samsung: samsung.com/in/recycling - call 1800-40-SAMSUNG
- Apple: apple.com/in/shop/trade-in
- Lenovo: lenovo.com/in/en/social_responsibility/recycling
- HP: hp.com/in-en/hp-information/recycling/learnmore.html
- All registered manufacturers must provide take-back under EPR

OPTION 2: AUTHORIZED E-WASTE COLLECTORS
Bangalore: Attibele Recycling Park, Exigo Recycling, E-Green (Peenya)
Delhi: Greenscape Eco Management, Ash Recyclers (Okhla)
Mumbai: Ecoreco, Trishyirec (Navi Mumbai)
Chennai: Ramky Enviro Engineers
Hyderabad: Ramky E-Waste, Greentek Reman

OPTION 3: MUNICIPAL DRIVES
- BBMP (Bangalore): Monthly e-waste drives in each ward - check bbmp.gov.in
- MCD (Delhi): e-waste collection at 14 collection centers across Delhi
- MCGM (Mumbai): e-waste collection vans in all 24 wards on designated days

BATTERIES (Special Rules):
- Li-ion (phone, laptop): Never puncture or throw in general waste. MANDATORY to return to manufacturer or authorized collector
- Lead-acid (car, inverter): Return to battery dealer - they are REQUIRED by law to take them back
- Alkaline (AA, AAA): Can go in dry waste if small quantity; better at collection centers

FLUORESCENT/CFL BULBS:
- Contain mercury - NEVER break. Wrap in paper, tape, place in RED hazardous bin
- LED bulbs: Dry waste or e-waste center
- Philips, Syska, Wipro have take-back programs

PENALTY FOR DUMPING E-WASTE:
- Household: Warning to Rs. 10,000
- Bulk generators (offices): Up to Rs. 1 lakh
- Recyclers without authorization: Up to Rs. 10 lakh + imprisonment
