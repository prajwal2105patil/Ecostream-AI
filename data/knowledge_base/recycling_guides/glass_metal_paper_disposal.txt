GLASS, METAL, AND PAPER DISPOSAL GUIDE FOR INDIA
SWM Rules 2016 Compliance — Blue Bin (Dry Recyclable Waste)

=== GLASS DISPOSAL ===
Glass is 100% recyclable indefinitely without quality loss — one of the most valuable recyclables.

WHAT GOES IN BLUE BIN (Glass):
- Glass bottles: water bottles, beer/alcohol bottles, soft drink bottles, wine bottles
- Glass jars: pickle jars, jam jars, honey jars, sauce jars, medicine jars
- Glass tumblers and cups (broken is okay — wrap in newspaper and label "BROKEN GLASS")
- Window glass (broken — wrap securely in newspaper, tape, label "SHARP")
- Mirror glass (wrap in newspaper)
- Cookware: Pyrex, borosilicate glass baking dishes

WHAT NOT IN BLUE BIN (Glass):
- Ceramics and porcelain (cups, plates, flower pots, tiles) — RED/reject bin; different melting point
- Crystal glassware with lead — RED bin
- Tempered glass (car windshields) — requires special handling, call kabadiwala
- CFL/fluorescent bulbs — e-waste channel (contain mercury)
- Light bulbs (incandescent) — RED bin
- Mirrors with silver backing — kabadiwala handles differently

HOW TO PREPARE GLASS FOR RECYCLING:
1. Empty all contents — rinse with water
2. Remove metal lids and caps (recycle separately as metal)
3. Remove paper labels (optional but preferred)
4. Do NOT crush or break intentionally (broken glass = injury risk for handlers)
5. Place in blue bin OR give to kabadiwala
6. If broken: wrap in 2-3 layers of newspaper, tape securely, label "BROKEN GLASS — SHARP"

WHERE GLASS GOES:
BBMP DWCC → Glass aggregator → Glass cullet manufacturer
Glass cullet (crushed glass) is sold back to glass manufacturers
Bangalore glass recyclers: Hind Glass Industries (Peenya), Virasat Glass (Rajajinagar)
Kabadiwala rate: Rs. 0–2 per kg (glass is heavy; small quantities often free)
Note: Kabadiwala buys glass in large quantities (>10 kg) — deposit at DWCC for small quantities

=== METAL DISPOSAL ===
Metals are among the most valuable recyclable materials — never landfill metal.

WHAT GOES IN BLUE BIN (Metal):
- Aluminium: cans (cold drink, beer), foil (fold into ball), aluminium containers, bottle caps
- Steel: tin cans (food tins — tomato paste, condensed milk), steel utensils (old tawa, cooker)
- Copper: old wire, plumbing pipes, motor windings — HIGH VALUE
- Iron/mild steel: nails, screws, brackets, iron rods (small), tools
- Zinc: galvanised iron sheets, old buckets
- Mixed metal: door handles, hinges, locks

WHAT NOT IN BLUE BIN (Metal):
- Batteries (lead-acid, lithium-ion, AA cells) — RED bin or e-waste channel
- Aerosol/spray cans with residual gas — let completely empty, puncture, then blue bin
- Paint tins with dried paint residue — RED bin (hazardous)
- Contaminated oil drums — needs industrial recycler

HOW TO PREPARE METAL FOR RECYCLING:
1. Empty cans completely; rinse food tins once
2. Crush cans to save space (aluminium cans are easy to crush by hand)
3. Separate metals by type if possible — kabadiwala pays more for sorted metal
4. Copper wire: strip plastic insulation if possible (kabadiwala pays 3–4x more for bare copper)
5. Large metal items (old fans, geysers, iron frames): Call kabadiwala for pickup

KABADIWALA RATES FOR METALS IN BANGALORE (2023 approximate):
- Aluminium: Rs. 80–100/kg (highest value recyclable!)
- Copper: Rs. 400–500/kg (bare wire), Rs. 200–250/kg (with insulation)
- Brass: Rs. 250–300/kg
- Stainless steel: Rs. 30–50/kg
- Iron/mild steel: Rs. 20–30/kg
- Tin cans: Rs. 10–15/kg
- Aluminium foil: Rs. 40–60/kg (clean)

WHERE METAL GOES:
Blue bin → DWCC → Scrap dealer → Metal smelter → Reused in manufacturing
Recycling aluminium saves 95% of energy compared to making new aluminium from bauxite
Recycling steel saves 60% energy vs. virgin steel production

=== PAPER AND CARDBOARD DISPOSAL ===
Paper is the most recycled material in India — strong kabadiwala network handles it efficiently.

WHAT GOES IN BLUE BIN (Paper/Cardboard):
- Newspapers, magazines, books (remove hardcovers if possible)
- Cardboard boxes (flatten to save space) — move boxes, Amazon/Flipkart delivery boxes
- Envelopes (remove plastic window if present)
- Office paper, printouts, notebooks
- Paper bags (non-food soiled)
- Cereal boxes, medicine boxes (remove foil liner if present)
- Paper cups (dry, food residue removed) — some DWCCs accept
- Tissue paper boxes (cardboard only, not tissue paper)

WHAT NOT IN BLUE BIN (Paper):
- Soiled paper (food stains, oil stains, used tissues, napkins) → GREEN bin (compostable)
- Thermal paper (receipts, ATM slips) — RED bin (contain BPA chemical, not recyclable)
- Carbon paper — RED bin
- Paper with heavy lamination or metallic coating — RED bin
- Wet/soggy paper — GREEN bin (compostable) or dry first
- Photograph paper — RED bin (chemical coating)
- Wax-coated paper — RED bin

HOW TO PREPARE PAPER FOR RECYCLING:
1. Keep paper DRY — wet paper degrades quality significantly
2. Flatten cardboard boxes — remove any styrofoam inserts
3. Remove staples and binding clips (leave is okay too)
4. Bundle newspapers together with string/rubber band
5. Store in a dry place until collection day

KABADIWALA RATES FOR PAPER IN BANGALORE (2023 approximate):
- Newspaper: Rs. 10–14/kg
- White office paper: Rs. 12–16/kg
- Cardboard/corrugated: Rs. 6–10/kg
- Books (mixed paper): Rs. 6–9/kg
- Magazines (glossy): Rs. 4–6/kg

=== COMBINED KABADIWALA GUIDE FOR BANGALORE ===
HOW TO CALL A KABADIWALA IN BANGALORE:
1. I Got Garbage App (most popular in Bangalore) — book pickup, get instant quote
2. Kabadiwala.com — online booking, minimum 5 kg
3. ScrapQ app — quote for metal, paper, plastic, electronics
4. Local kabadiwala: Ask neighbours, check near vegetable markets, sabzi mandis
5. BBMP DWCC: Drop recyclables and kabadiwala at DWCC buys from you

BEST PRACTICE:
- Collect 2–4 weeks of recyclables before calling kabadiwala — builds up quantity
- Sort by material type: paper separate, metal separate, plastic separate
- Kabadiwala gives more money for sorted/clean materials
- Keep receipt for large transactions (Rs. 500+) for tax purposes (if running a business)
