ORGANIC WASTE AND COMPOSTING GUIDE FOR INDIA

WHAT IS ORGANIC/WET WASTE?
- Fruit and vegetable peels and scraps
- Cooked and uncooked food waste
- Eggshells (considered dry but can compost)
- Tea bags and coffee grounds
- Garden waste: leaves, flowers, grass clippings
- Wilted/old flowers
- Paper napkins (wet/food-stained)

WHAT NOT TO PUT IN ORGANIC BIN:
- Meat, fish, bones (attracts pests - better in biogas)
- Dairy products in large quantities
- Oily/greasy food in large amounts
- Diseased plants

DISPOSAL OPTIONS IN INDIA:

1. POURAKARMIKAS / DOOR-TO-DOOR COLLECTION
- Separate wet waste in green bag/bin for daily collection
- Collection time: 6-10 AM in most Indian cities
- Do NOT mix wet with dry - leads to entire batch going to landfill

2. HOME COMPOSTING
Equipment: Earthen pot, plastic bin with holes, terrace composter
Process:
- Layer 1: Brown material (dry leaves, cardboard strips)
- Layer 2: Green material (food waste)
- Add microbial culture or cow dung for faster decomposition
- Turn every 3-5 days, maintain moisture (damp not wet)
- Ready in 30-45 days
BBMP subsidy: Rs. 500 for composting units
Output: Rich organic fertilizer for garden/terrace garden

3. BIOGAS PLANTS (Apartment Complex Scale)
- Minimum: 50+ families for a community biogas unit
- Output: Cooking gas + organic slurry fertilizer
- BBMP provides capital subsidy: 50% up to Rs. 1 lakh
- Bangalore has 200+ apartment biogas plants

4. KHAMBA / TERRACE COMPOSTING
- Suitable for 1-4 person households
- Three-tier system: Top tier (fresh waste), Middle (decomposing), Bottom (ready compost)
- Available from BBMP for Rs. 500 (actual cost Rs. 2000)

COMPOSTING DO'S AND DON'TS:
DO: Add dry material to balance wet, turn regularly, keep covered
DON'T: Add plastics, glass, metal, synthetic fibers, treated wood

VERMICOMPOSTING:
- Uses earthworms (Eisenia fetida) to break down organic matter
- Produces worm castings - the most nutrient-rich organic fertilizer
- Suitable for home use, requires 0.5 sq.ft space
- GKVK and IIHR Bangalore sell starter kits

IMPACT OF ORGANIC WASTE IN LANDFILLS:
- India sends 60% of waste (mostly organic) to landfills
- Organic matter in landfills produces methane (28x more potent greenhouse gas than CO2)
- Every 1 kg composted at home prevents 0.5 kg CO2-equivalent emissions

PENALTY:
- Bulk organic waste generators (hotels >10 kg/day) must compost on-site
- Fine: Rs. 5000/day for non-compliance under SWM Rules 2016
