PLASTIC DISPOSAL IN BANGALORE AND INDIA — COMPLETE GUIDE

=== IDENTIFYING PLASTIC TYPES (Resin Code on bottom of container) ===

#1 PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate) — HIGHLY RECYCLABLE
Examples: Water bottles, cold drink bottles, juice bottles, cooking oil bottles, mouthwash bottles
Bin: BLUE (Dry recyclable)
Steps:
1. Empty completely — no liquid residue
2. Rinse with water once
3. Crush the bottle to save space
4. Remove cap and dispose separately (cap = #5 PP)
5. Remove paper label if possible
6. Place in blue bin or hand to kabadiwala
Where it goes: DWCC → kabadiwala → plastic granule manufacturer (PET recycled into polyester fibre, new bottles)
Kabadiwala rate: Rs. 8–12 per kg in Bangalore (2023)

#2 HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene) — HIGHLY RECYCLABLE
Examples: Milk bottles, shampoo bottles, detergent containers, motor oil cans, water cans
Bin: BLUE (Dry recyclable)
Steps:
1. Empty and rinse thoroughly (especially oil/chemical containers)
2. Remove pump dispensers (non-recyclable — red bin)
3. Crush and place in blue bin
Kabadiwala rate: Rs. 6–10 per kg in Bangalore

#3 PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) — DIFFICULT TO RECYCLE, HANDLE WITH CARE
Examples: Pipes, window frames, cable insulation, synthetic leather bags, shower curtains, blister packs
Bin: BLUE (dry bin — some facilities accept, others don't)
NEVER BURN PVC — releases dioxins and hydrochloric acid gas, extremely toxic
Steps: Place in blue bin; if DWCC rejects, place in red bin for special disposal
Note: PVC is the most hazardous plastic to recycle — avoid purchasing PVC products where possible

#4 LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene) — PARTIALLY RECYCLABLE
Examples: Plastic carry bags, cling wrap, bread bags, squeeze bottles, plastic film packaging, bubble wrap
Bin: BLUE bin (but many DWCCs don't accept loose plastic film)
Steps:
1. Collect clean plastic bags in a single bag
2. Hand the bag-of-bags to kabadiwala
3. Plastic film: fold flat and bundle together
Note: India's Plastic Waste Management Rules 2022 ban single-use plastic bags <75 microns
Alternatives: Cloth bags (reusable), paper bags, jute bags

#5 PP (Polypropylene) — RECYCLABLE
Examples: Yogurt containers, medicine bottles, bottle caps, straws, microwave containers, storage boxes, plastic chairs
Bin: BLUE (dry recyclable)
Steps:
1. Rinse food containers
2. Keep bottle caps on — they are #5 PP
3. Place in blue bin or give to kabadiwala
Kabadiwala rate: Rs. 4–8 per kg

#6 PS (Polystyrene/Thermocol) — MOSTLY NOT RECYCLABLE
Examples: Thermocol packaging, disposable plates and cups, egg cartons, foam food containers
Bin: RED bin (reject waste — cannot be recycled easily)
NEVER BURN thermocol — releases styrene gas, a known carcinogen
Disposal: Compact, bag, and place in red/reject bin for landfill disposal
India banned expanded polystyrene (thermocol) as single-use plastic from July 2022

#7 Other (PC, ABS, Nylon, Bioplastic, Mixed)
Examples: CDs/DVDs, hard plastic toys, 3- and 5-gallon water jugs, electronic casings, sunglasses, baby bottles
Bin: BLUE bin (hard plastic) or RED bin (if unsure)
Note: Polycarbonate (#7) may contain BPA — do not heat food in these containers
Electronic casings: Treat as e-waste, hand to authorised e-waste recycler

=== INDIA'S PLASTIC WASTE MANAGEMENT RULES 2022 ===
Single-Use Plastic (SUP) items BANNED in India from July 1, 2022:
- Plastic sticks for balloons
- Plastic sticks in ear buds/cotton swabs
- Plastic flags
- Candy/lollipop sticks
- Ice cream sticks
- Polystyrene (thermocol) for decoration
- Plastic plates, cups, glasses, cutlery (forks, spoons, knives)
- Plastic straws
- Plastic trays
- Plastic wrapping/packaging film around sweet boxes
- Invitation cards with plastic coating
- Cigarette packets with plastic wrapping
- Carry bags below 75 microns thickness
- Plastic stirrers for beverages

Penalty for manufacturing/using banned SUP: Rs. 10,000–1,00,000 per offence (EPR violation: cancellation of registration)

=== HOW TO DISPOSE PLASTIC IN BANGALORE ===
STEP 1: Check the resin code (triangle with number) on the bottom
STEP 2: Segregate — recyclable (#1,2,4,5) go in blue bin; thermocol, PVC in red bin
STEP 3: Rinse containers — dirty plastic is rejected by recyclers
STEP 4: Hand to Pourakarmika or deposit at DWCC
STEP 5: For large quantities (moving house, renovation) — contact kabadiwala directly

FINDING A KABADIWALA IN BANGALORE:
- Kabadiwala.com app — online pickup service, minimum 5 kg
- I Got Garbage app — Bangalore-specific, book pickup online
- ScrapQ app — instant quote, doorstep pickup
- Local kabadiwala shops: every area has one; often in small lanes near markets

=== MULTILAYER PLASTIC (MLP) ===
Examples: Chips packets, namkeen packets, biscuit wrappers, chocolate wrappers, toothpaste tubes
These are NOT recyclable through normal channels
Bin: RED bin (reject waste)
EPR Programme: Many FMCG brands have take-back programmes for MLP — check brand website
Alternative: Some organisations (Kabadiwala Foundation, Saahas) collect MLP for co-processing in cement kilns

=== PLASTIC BOTTLES — STEP BY STEP (QUICK REFERENCE) ===
Water bottle (PET): Empty → rinse → crush → blue bin ✓
Milk pouch (LDPE): Cut open → rinse → blue bin (bundle with other pouches)
Oil bottle (HDPE): Empty → rinse thoroughly → blue bin ✓
Shampoo bottle: Squeeze out completely → rinse → remove pump → blue bin ✓
Ketchup bottle: Empty → rinse → remove cap → blue bin ✓
Medicine strip (blister pack): Red bin (PVC + aluminium laminate — not recyclable)
Chips packet: Red bin (MLP — not recyclable)
Thermocol box: Red bin — NEVER BURN ✓
