PLASTIC WASTE DISPOSAL GUIDE FOR INDIAN HOUSEHOLDS

IDENTIFYING PLASTIC TYPES (Resin Code on bottom of container):

#1 PET (Polyethylene Terephthalate)
- Examples: Water bottles, cold drink bottles, juice bottles, mouthwash bottles
- Recyclability: HIGH - Most valuable recyclable plastic in India
- Bin: Blue Dry Waste bin
- Preparation: Empty, rinse, crush to save space, remove caps (caps can go separately)
- Kabadiwala value: Rs. 6-10 per kg

#2 HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene)
- Examples: Milk pouches, shampoo bottles, detergent bottles, buckets
- Recyclability: HIGH
- Bin: Blue Dry Waste bin
- Preparation: Rinse thoroughly, remove labels if possible
- Kabadiwala value: Rs. 5-8 per kg

#3 PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)
- Examples: Some pipes, floor tiles, some packaging
- Recyclability: LOW - Toxic when burned, avoid
- Bin: Dry Waste Collection Centre (DWCC) only - do NOT give to kabadiwala
- Note: Contains phthalates and lead - handle carefully

#4 LDPE (Low-Density Polyethylene)
- Examples: Plastic bags, cling film, bread bags, squeeze bottles
- Recyclability: MEDIUM - Accepted at select DWCCs
- Bin: Dry Waste Collection Centre
- Note: NEVER recycle plastic bags with food residue - contaminates batch

#5 PP (Polypropylene)
- Examples: Yogurt containers, medicine bottles, bottle caps, straws, chips bags
- Recyclability: MEDIUM-HIGH
- Bin: Blue Dry Waste bin
- Preparation: Rinse and dry

#6 PS (Polystyrene/Thermocol)
- Examples: Thermocol packaging, foam cups, takeout containers
- Recyclability: VERY LOW in India - not accepted by most facilities
- Bin: Red Reject bin in BBMP; General waste in other cities
- Note: Never burn thermocol - releases styrene, a carcinogen

#7 Other (Mixed)
- Examples: Multi-layer plastic, some water cooler bottles, CDs
- Recyclability: VERY LOW
- Bin: Red Reject / Dry Waste Collection Centre
- Multi-layer plastic (chips packets, juice boxes): DWCC or authorized collector

IMPORTANT RULES FOR ALL PLASTIC:
1. The "Greasy Pizza Box Rule": If plastic has food contamination (oil, curry), it cannot be recycled - put in wet waste
2. BLACK PLASTIC cannot be detected by sorting machines - goes to landfill even if "recyclable" type
3. Always separate caps from bottles (different plastic types)
4. The number inside the triangle does NOT mean it's recycled - check with your local DWCC

KABADIWALA NETWORK:
- Informal collectors are India's backbone of plastic recycling
- Accept: PET bottles, HDPE, newspapers, metal, glass bottles
- Do NOT accept: Multi-layer, food-contaminated, dirty plastic
- App: Kabadiwala app available in Bangalore, Pune, Delhi for scheduled pickup
