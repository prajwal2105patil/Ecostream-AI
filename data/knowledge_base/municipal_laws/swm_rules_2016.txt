SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT RULES 2016 - INDIA
Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change, Government of India

KEY PROVISIONS FOR CITIZENS:

1. MANDATORY SEGREGATION AT SOURCE
All households must segregate waste into three categories:
- WET WASTE (Green Bin): Food scraps, vegetable peels, fruit waste, garden waste, cooked food, teabags, flowers, leaves
- DRY WASTE (Blue Bin): Paper, cardboard, plastic bottles, glass, metal cans, cartons, rubber, cloth, wood
- HAZARDOUS WASTE (Red Bin): Batteries, bulbs, medicines, chemicals, e-waste, paint cans, syringes, diapers, sanitary napkins

2. PENALTY FOR NON-COMPLIANCE
- Throwing unsegregated waste: Fine up to Rs. 500 per instance
- Littering in public: Fine Rs. 200 to Rs. 1000
- Burning waste: Fine up to Rs. 5000
- Municipal authorities can impose higher penalties

3. EXTENDED PRODUCER RESPONSIBILITY (EPR)
Manufacturers of plastic, e-waste, and packaging must take back their products.
Citizens can return e-waste to authorized collection centers or manufacturer take-back programs.

4. BULK WASTE GENERATORS
Hotels, offices, markets with >100 kg/day must arrange own collection and composting.

5. PLASTIC WASTE
- Single-use plastic below 75 microns is banned in India (effective July 2022)
- Multi-layer plastic sachets must go to Dry Waste Collection Centre (DWCC)
- PET bottles (water, cola) are highly recyclable - rinse before disposal

6. ORGANIC WASTE
- Wet waste should be composted at home or sent to biogas plants
- Do not mix wet waste with dry waste - contamination makes recycling impossible
- Garden waste (leaves, branches) should be composted separately

7. E-WASTE (Electronic Waste)
- NEVER throw in regular bins - contains heavy metals (lead, mercury, cadmium)
- Drop at authorized E-Waste Collection Centers
- Contact manufacturer for free take-back programs (mandatory under E-Waste Rules 2022)
- BBMP (Bangalore), MCD (Delhi), MCGM (Mumbai) have designated collection points

8. BIOMEDICAL WASTE
- Used masks, syringes, bandages with blood: dispose in Red bag at hospitals or BBMP hazardous collection
- Citizens should use yellow/red designated bags for COVID/biomedical waste
- Municipal medical waste must go to authorized Treatment Storage Disposal Facility (TSDF)

9. CONSTRUCTION AND DEMOLITION WASTE
- Must be separately collected and sent to C&D waste processing facilities
- Cannot be mixed with regular solid waste
- Contact local municipal body for bulky C&D pickup

10. COMPOSTING
- Home composting of wet waste is encouraged and subsidized in many Indian cities
- BBMP Bangalore: Subsidized composting units available for Rs. 500 (actual cost Rs. 1500)
- Terrace composting and kitchen composting bins available from municipal bodies
