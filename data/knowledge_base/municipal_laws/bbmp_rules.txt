BBMP (BRUHAT BENGALURU MAHANAGARA PALIKE) SOLID WASTE MANAGEMENT GUIDELINES
Bangalore, Karnataka, India — Updated 2023

=== BBMP THREE-BIN SYSTEM (MANDATORY) ===
BBMP mandates three-bin segregation for ALL Bangalore households and commercial establishments:

GREEN BIN — WET/BIODEGRADABLE WASTE:
- Vegetable and fruit peels, scraps, seeds
- Cooked and uncooked food leftovers
- Eggshells, fish bones, meat scraps
- Tea bags, coffee grounds, filter paper
- Dry leaves, garden trimmings, flower waste
- Soiled paper (food-soiled napkins, paper bags)
- Coconut shells, sugarcane bagasse

BLUE BIN — DRY/RECYCLABLE WASTE:
- Plastic bottles and containers (PET, HDPE, PP) — rinse before discarding
- Cardboard, newspapers, magazines, books
- Metal tins, aluminium foil, steel scrap
- Glass bottles and jars
- Clean cloth and rubber items
- Tetra packs (milk cartons, juice boxes)
- Plastic bags (clean and dry)

RED BIN — REJECT/HAZARDOUS WASTE:
- Sanitary napkins and diapers (wrapped in newspaper)
- Medicines and syringes
- Batteries (all types)
- CFL/fluorescent bulbs
- Cosmetics, personal care products (non-recyclable)
- Thermocol, carbon paper, sticker labels
- Soiled bandages and cotton

=== BBMP COLLECTION SCHEDULE — BANGALORE ===
DOOR-TO-DOOR COLLECTION:
- Residential areas: 6:00 AM to 10:00 AM daily (Pourakarmikas with 3-compartment vehicles)
- Commercial areas: 7:00 AM to 11:00 AM daily
- Bulk generators: Separate schedule — contact BBMP ward office

SECONDARY COLLECTION POINTS:
- Mini Transfer Stations (MTS): located in each ward — accept segregated waste
- Auto-tippers: operate in narrow lanes not accessible to collection vehicles
- BBMP helpline for missed collection: 1533 (toll-free, Bangalore)

=== DRY WASTE COLLECTION CENTRES (DWCC) ===
BBMP operates 208 DWCCs across Bangalore (target: one per ward).
Timings: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM, Monday–Saturday (closed Sunday)
Accepted at DWCC:
- All dry recyclables (paper, plastic, metal, glass, cardboard, e-waste)
- Furniture (small items)
- Kabadiwala services available at most DWCCs — get paid for recyclables!

=== BBMP BULK GENERATOR RULES ===
Any premises generating >100 kg waste/day is classified as Bulk Generator:
Includes: Apartments (>50 units), hotels, restaurants, offices, malls, hospitals
Obligations:
1. Must process wet waste ON-SITE using composting unit or biogas plant
2. Compost/biogas slurry can be used as manure in gardens
3. Dry waste must be handed to registered recyclers
4. Monthly waste audit report to BBMP zonal office
5. Fine: Rs. 10,000/month for non-compliance + vehicle impoundment for illegal dumping

=== BBMP APARTMENT COMPLEX RULES ===
Housing societies and apartment complexes:
1. Must install wet waste processing unit (minimum 1 unit per 50 households)
2. Compost bins in garden areas mandatory
3. Separate storage room for dry recyclables mandatory
4. Awareness drives for residents — BBMP provides training on request
5. Best performer: "Clean and Green" certification from BBMP
6. Incentive: Property tax rebate of 5% for certified green apartments

=== BBMP E-WASTE COLLECTION ===
Special e-waste collection drives: First Saturday of every month at ward offices
Accepted items: Mobile phones, laptops, tablets, chargers, wires, TVs, fans, geysers
BBMP authorised e-waste recyclers in Bangalore:
- Attero Recycling, Sarjapur Road
- E-Parisaraa Pvt Ltd, Dobbespet
- Ash Recyclers, Peenya Industrial Area
Drop-off: Any DWCC accepts e-waste; collected and sent to authorised recyclers weekly

=== BBMP PENALTIES — BANGALORE SPECIFIC ===
- Not segregating waste: Rs. 200 (first notice), Rs. 500 (second notice), Rs. 1,000 + court summons (third)
- Throwing garbage in public: Rs. 500 on-the-spot fine
- Illegal dumping on roads/vacant sites: Rs. 5,000–25,000
- Commercial establishments not using three bins: Rs. 500/day fine
- Burning waste: Rs. 5,000 + remediation cost
- Apartments not composting: Rs. 10,000/month

=== BBMP POURAKARMIKAS ===
Pourakarmikas are BBMP's sanitation workers who collect door-to-door:
- Always hand waste in SEGREGATED form — wet, dry, hazardous separately
- Do NOT mix and hand over as single bag — they are not allowed to accept mixed waste
- Tip: Keep a cloth bag for dry waste, bucket for wet waste, small bin for red bin items
- Pourakarmikas work 6 days/week; Sunday is rest day (no collection most areas)
- If collection is missed, call 1533 or register complaint on BBMP portal

=== BBMP COMPOSTING INITIATIVE ===
BBMP Sahaaya Composting Programme:
- Free compost bins distributed to apartments > 20 units
- Master Composters programme: BBMP trains residents for free
- Compost produced: Citizen can use in garden or donate to BBMP's urban farming initiative
- Biomethanation plants: 11 BBMP plants processing ~600 TPD wet waste into electricity/biogas
- Major composting sites: Doddabidarakallu (350 TPD), Lingadheeranahalli (350 TPD), Mavallipura
